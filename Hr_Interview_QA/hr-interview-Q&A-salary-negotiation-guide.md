# 📊 2026 DevOps Salary Benchmarks (India)

Based on market trends across product companies, funded startups, and service-based MNCs in 2026:

## 🔹 Service-Based Companies

• Entry-Level DevOps (0–1 year): ₹4.5 – ₹7 LPA
• DevOps (1–2 years): ₹6 – ₹9 LPA

## 🔹 Funded Startups / Mid-Size Tech Companies

• Entry-Level DevOps: ₹6 – ₹10 LPA
• DevOps (1–2 years strong hands-on): ₹8 – ₹14 LPA

## 🔹 Product-Based Companies / SaaS Companies

• Entry-Level DevOps: ₹8 – ₹14 LPA
• DevOps (1–2 years skilled in Kubernetes + Terraform + CI/CD): ₹12 – ₹20 LPA

## 🔹 Remote / Global Startups

• ₹12 – ₹25 LPA (skill-dependent)

Note: High-end range applies only if you have real production exposure (CI/CD pipelines, EKS, Terraform modules, monitoring, incident handling).

# 🎯 Core Positioning Strategy

You are NOT negotiating as:

❌ "Someone earning ₹1.8 LPA asking for a hike"

You ARE negotiating as:

✅ "A DevOps Engineer with deployable production skills seeking market correction"

Percentage hike from a low base salary is misleading.
Market alignment is what matters.

---

# 💬 HR Salary Negotiation – Structured Answers

---

## 1️⃣ HR: "Your current CTC is ₹1.8L. Why are you asking ₹6–7L?"

**Professional Answer:**

> My current compensation reflects my initial entry-level opportunity. However, over the past year, I’ve built strong hands-on experience in AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, and CI/CD automation.
> I’m now capable of independently handling deployments, infrastructure automation, and monitoring setups.
> Based on 2026 market standards for DevOps engineers with deployable skills, ₹6–7 LPA aligns with industry benchmarks.

---

## 2️⃣ HR: "That’s almost a 4x hike. That’s too much."

**Strong Response:**

> I understand it appears large percentage-wise. However, percentage hike from a low base salary can be misleading.
> What matters more is market correction. For DevOps engineers with containerization, automation, and CI/CD experience, ₹6–7 LPA falls within standard compensation ranges in service and startup environments.

---

## 3️⃣ HR: "We can offer ₹5 LPA."

**Negotiation Counter:**

> Thank you for the offer. I genuinely appreciate it. Based on the responsibilities discussed and my skill alignment, I was targeting closer to ₹6.5 LPA.
> Is there flexibility in the compensation structure, such as performance bonus or structured revision?

---

## 4️⃣ HR: "Final offer ₹5.5 LPA."

**Strategic Acceptance Condition:**

> If there is a structured performance review within 6 months with a salary revision based on measurable performance milestones, I would be comfortable proceeding.
